Understanding Demand Charges

Types of Charges:

Customer Charge: This is the fee for being connected to the grid.

Energy Charges: These are charges based on the actual energy consumed during a reporting period. ($1.43 per day)

Peak Period:

Definition: High-demand times of the day.

Timeframe: Typically during business hours or early evenings.

Higher Costs: Electricity rates are higher during peaks. (typical $0.23 / kwH for Achadinna)

Demand Charges: Contributes to additional charges for maximum usage.

Example: 5 PM to 8 PM on weekdays.

Non-Peak Period:

Definition: Low-demand times, like nighttime or weekends.

Lower Costs: Electricity rates are usually lower. ($0.19/ kwH)
